Third assignment: random failures and target attacks

In this third assignment you will investigate the robustness of networks by simulating random failures and target attacks. The assignment is composed of two parts.

Part 1: Use synthetic graphs

  1. In the first exercise, you can use graphs obtained by using the networx library. See how many graph generators are available in this library: https://networkx.github.io/documentation/development/reference/generators.html
  2. For each selected graph (max 2 or 3) you can perform different types of attack: turn off nodes at random, turn off the highest degree nodes, those with the highest pagerank, those with the highest clustering coefficient, ...
  3. After each removal, compute new measures, for example the size of the giant component or the diameter of the network and then plot these measures with respect to node failures.
  4. Be careful, some of the networkx functions you will use work only for undirected, connected graphs and therefore you need to instrument your code to work on the entire graph first, and on the several components after the split of the original graph.

Part 1.alt: Conspiracy in Social Networks

(From Barabasi book, alternative to the exercise in Part 1)

In a Big Brother society, the thought police wants to follow a "divide and conquer" strategy by fragmenting the social network into isolated components. You belong to the resistance and want to foil their plans. There are rumours that the police wants to detain individuals that have many friends and individuals whose friends tend to know each other. The resistance puts you in charge to decide which individuals to protect: those whose friendship circle is highly interconnected or those with many friends. To decide you simulate two different attacks on your network, by removing (i) the nodes that have the highest clustering coefficient and (ii) the nodes that have the largest degree.

Study the size of the giant component in function of the fraction of removed nodes for the two attacks on the following networks:

  1. A network with N = 104 nodes generated and power-law degree distribution with γ = 2.5.
  2. A network with N = 104 nodes generated with the hierarchical model

Part 2: Use your real graph

  1. Repeat the attacks of Part 1 also with the graph you have computed/analysed for the first assignment.

For the delivery, write a short document describing the attacks and the results you have obtained.

Be careful, your own graph is not a complete graph and therefore the results you will obtain are not "universal" but depend on the crawling strategy you have implemented and on the stop criteria you (or the authors of the dataset you have chosen) have defined.
